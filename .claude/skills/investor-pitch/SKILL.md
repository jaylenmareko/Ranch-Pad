# Investor Pitch Skill

Build pitch decks that get meetings, not polite passes. Structure, story, and substance — all three matter.

---

## Before Starting

Gather context (ask if not provided):

1. **Stage** — Pre-seed, seed, Series A, Series B?
2. **Ask** — How much are you raising? At what valuation (or range)?
3. **Use of funds** — What does the money unlock specifically?
4. **Traction** — What have you proven so far? (Revenue, users, pilots, LOIs)
5. **Audience** — Specific investor or fund type? (Angel, VC, strategic)

---

## The 10-Slide Core Structure

This is the order that works. Don't reorder it without a reason.

### 1. Problem
- The specific pain. Make it visceral — not "the market is inefficient."
- Who feels it? How often? What's the cost of the status quo?
- One crisp sentence: "X people/businesses suffer Y problem every Z, costing them $W."

### 2. Solution
- Your answer to the problem above. One sentence, plain language.
- NOT a list of features. A transformation: "We turn X into Y."
- Show don't tell — demo screenshot, before/after, or 30-second flow

### 3. Market Size
- TAM / SAM / SOM — bottom-up preferred over top-down
- Bottom-up: # of customers × ACV = SAM. Show your math.
- Investors want SAM > $1B for VC. Be honest about your real addressable market.

### 4. Product
- 3-4 key workflows, not a feature tour
- Map to customer pain from slide 1
- If B2B: show the buying persona's workflow. If B2C: show the moment of value.

### 5. Business Model
- How you make money — pricing, tiers, transaction vs. subscription
- ACV / ARPU, gross margin, expansion revenue potential
- Keep it simple: one or two revenue streams max

### 6. Traction
- The most important slide for seed+
- Revenue, MRR growth, DAU/MAU, retention, NPS, pilot customers, LOIs
- Trend matters more than absolute number — show the line going up
- If pre-revenue: design partners, waitlist, pilot agreements, usage data

### 7. Go-To-Market
- First 12 months: how do you get your first 100 customers?
- Channel strategy: paid, outbound, partnerships, product-led
- Unit economics: CAC, LTV, CAC payback period
- Don't say "we'll go viral" — show a repeatable motion

### 8. Competition
- 2x2 matrix or feature table — you win on the axes that matter most to your buyer
- Don't say "no competition" — it signals you haven't done the work
- Name the real alternatives (including "do nothing" / spreadsheets)

### 9. Team
- Why you? Why now? Why this specific problem?
- Domain expertise, prior exits, relevant failures, unfair advantages
- Show the gap if there is one — and what you're doing about it

### 10. The Ask
- Amount, use of funds (by category), key milestones it funds
- Timeline to next milestone / next raise
- What does success look like in 18 months?

## Storytelling Principles

### The Narrative Arc
Every great pitch tells one story: **The world has a broken problem → you discovered a better way → here's proof it works → here's how to capture the market.**

### The "So What" Test
After every slide, a skeptical investor asks "so what?" Answer it before they can.

### Specificity > Generality
"We target mid-market ranching operations with 200-500 head that use paper records" beats "we target the agriculture market."

### Don't Pitch Features, Pitch Outcomes
"Ranchers catch disease 3 days earlier, saving $400/head" beats "AI-powered health monitoring."

## Investor Q&A Prep

Prepare for these — they will be asked:

| Question | What They're Really Asking |
|----------|--------------------------|
| "What's your moat?" | Why can't a big player copy this in 6 months? |
| "Why now?" | What changed that makes this the right moment? |
| "How do you know customers want this?" | Do you have evidence beyond your own conviction? |
| "What keeps you up at night?" | Do you know your real risks? |
| "What does the $X get you?" | Is this money enough to prove the hypothesis? |
| "Why are you the right team?" | Do you have an unfair advantage? |
| "What happens if [big competitor] does this?" | How defensible is your position? |

## Common Pitch Mistakes

- **Too much product, not enough problem** — Investors buy the problem, then evaluate the solution
- **Vanity metrics** — App downloads without DAU. Signups without activation. Leads without revenue.
- **"We just need 1% of a massive market"** — Bottom-up or nothing
- **No clear ask** — Vague "raising up to $X" without use of funds
- **Team slide last** — At pre-seed, team is often the investment thesis
- **Reading the slides** — The deck supports the conversation, not replaces it
- **Fake urgency** — "We have other term sheets" only works if you actually do

## Investor Outreach

**Cold outreach to VCs:**
- Lead with traction, not product
- One specific connection to their thesis/portfolio
- 4-5 lines max, link to deck
- Ask for 20 minutes, not a partner meeting

**Warm intro path:**
- Map your cap table and advisors → their networks → target funds
- A warm intro is 10x more likely to convert than cold

**Timing:**
- Raise when you have momentum, not when you're desperate
- Best times: January-May, September-October (avoid summer and holidays)
