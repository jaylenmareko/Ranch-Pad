# Hiring Skill

Build the team that builds the product. From first contractor to first full-time hire — structured, fast, and fair.

---

## Before Starting

1. **Stage** — First hire ever, or adding to existing team?
2. **Role type** — Contractor, part-time, or full-time employee?
3. **Urgency** — Fill a gap now, or strategic role?
4. **Budget** — What can you pay? (salary range or hourly)
5. **Location** — Remote, local, or hybrid?

---

## Contractor vs. Full-Time Employee

| Factor | Contractor | FTE |
|--------|-----------|-----|
| Speed to hire | Fast (days-weeks) | Slow (weeks-months) |
| Cost | Higher hourly, no benefits | Lower hourly, add ~25% for benefits/taxes |
| Control | Limited (deliverables only) | Full (hours, methods, tools) |
| Risk | Low (end contract anytime) | Higher (at-will but still complex) |
| Tax | 1099, they pay self-employment tax | W2, you withhold and match |
| Equity | Typically not | Yes |
| Best for | Specific project, skill gap, part-time need | Core team, long-term strategic role |

**Rule of thumb:** Start with contractors until you know the role is permanent. Convert to FTE when you need full-time commitment or the person is core to the mission.

## Job Description Template

```markdown
## [Role Title] — [Company Name]

**Type:** Full-time / Part-time / Contract
**Location:** Remote / [City] / Hybrid
**Compensation:** $X–$Y [salary or hourly]

### What you'll do
[3-5 bullet points. Specific outcomes, not vague responsibilities.]
- Build and own [specific thing]
- Work directly with [who] to [what outcome]
- Ship [X] within [timeframe]

### What we're looking for
[Skills and experience. Separate must-haves from nice-to-haves.]

Must have:
- [Non-negotiable 1]
- [Non-negotiable 2]

Nice to have:
- [Bonus 1]
- [Bonus 2]

### What you won't find here
[Be honest about constraints — stage, salary limits, etc. Saves everyone time.]

### About us
[2-3 sentences. Problem you're solving, stage, why it matters.]

### How to apply
[Specific instructions. Work sample, email format, etc.]
```

**Job description rules:**
- List compensation — always. Hiding it wastes everyone's time.
- Keep requirements ruthlessly short. Every extra requirement filters out good candidates.
- Be honest about stage and constraints. Surprises cause early turnover.

## Interview Process

### Lean process for early-stage (3 rounds max)

1. **Screening call (30 min)** — Mutual fit check, compensation alignment, role clarity
2. **Work sample / take-home (2-4 hours)** — See how they actually work
3. **Final interview (60 min)** — Values, culture, deep dive on work sample

### Work Sample Design

The work sample should be:
- Representative of the actual job (not trick questions)
- Completable in under 4 hours (respect their time)
- Paid if you're at revenue ($50-100 honorarium minimum)
- Evaluated with a scoring rubric before you see submissions

**Example work samples by role:**
- Engineer: Small bug fix + implement a feature in a repo you provide
- Designer: Redesign one screen of your product and explain decisions
- Marketer: Write a cold email sequence for your actual ICP
- Customer success: Draft an outreach email for a churned customer scenario

### Interview Question Framework

| Category | Sample Questions |
|----------|-----------------|
| Past behavior | "Tell me about a time you [specific situation]. What happened?" |
| Skills | "Walk me through how you would approach [specific problem]" |
| Values | "Describe a work environment where you do your best work" |
| Self-awareness | "What's something you're actively trying to improve at?" |
| Red flag check | "Why are you leaving [current role]?" |

**Never ask:**
- Hypotheticals ("What would you do if...") — past behavior predicts future behavior better
- Brain teasers — they test nothing relevant
- Age, family status, national origin, religion — illegal in the US

## Making an Offer

**Verbal offer first** (before written) — gauge acceptance likelihood before paperwork.

**Offer components:**
- Base salary or hourly rate
- Equity (if applicable) — % or shares, vesting schedule, cliff
- Benefits (health, dental, vision, 401k)
- PTO / time off policy
- Start date
- Remote/equipment stipend

**Offer letter musts:**
- At-will employment statement
- Confidentiality / IP assignment agreement
- Clear start date and compensation terms
- Benefits summary with effective date

**Negotiation approach:**
- Set a range you mean ($80K-$95K, not $80K-$120K)
- Know your walk-away number before the call
- Non-salary levers: equity, signing bonus, title, remote flexibility, start date

## Onboarding Plan

The first 30 days determine whether a hire stays.

### Week 1: Context
- Access to all tools, systems, accounts on Day 1 (not Day 3)
- 1:1 with you: expectations, communication style, how decisions are made
- Meet relevant team members / advisors
- Read key docs: CLAUDE.md, CONTEXT.md, relevant project docs

### Week 2-3: Contribution
- First small deliverable (low-stakes, builds confidence)
- Shadow customer calls or user sessions
- Access to production environment (read-only initially)

### Day 30: Check-in
- What's been confusing or missing?
- What would make them more effective?
- Do they feel set up to succeed?

## First Hire Checklist (Legal/Admin)

- [ ] EIN (employer ID) — register with IRS
- [ ] State employer registration
- [ ] Payroll setup (Gusto, Rippling, or ADP)
- [ ] Workers' comp insurance
- [ ] Employment contract + IP assignment agreement
- [ ] I-9 verification (employment eligibility)
- [ ] Benefits setup (health insurance if offering)

**For contractors only:**
- [ ] Contractor agreement (scope, payment terms, IP ownership)
- [ ] W-9 on file
- [ ] 1099 at year end if paid >$600
