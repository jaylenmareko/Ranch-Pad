# Customer Success Skill

Turn customers into advocates. Reduce churn, drive expansion, and build a repeatable CS motion.

---

## Before Starting

Understand the context:
1. **Business model** — B2B or B2C? Self-serve or high-touch?
2. **Customer segment** — SMB, mid-market, enterprise?
3. **Current CS maturity** — No CS function, early-stage, or scaling?
4. **Key problem** — Onboarding, churn, expansion, or all three?

---

## Customer Health Scoring

Build a health score before you can manage at scale.

**Input signals:**

| Signal | Weight | Healthy | At-Risk |
|--------|--------|---------|---------|
| Product usage (DAU/WAU) | High | Weekly active | No login 14+ days |
| Feature adoption | High | 3+ core features used | Only 1 feature |
| Support tickets | Medium | Low volume, resolved fast | High volume, escalations |
| NPS / CSAT | Medium | 8-10 | 0-6 |
| Contract renewal date | High | >90 days out | <30 days out |
| Stakeholder engagement | Medium | Multiple contacts active | Single contact only |
| Payments | High | Current | Overdue |

**Health tiers:**
- Green (70-100) — Expand and advocate
- Yellow (40-69) — Nurture and monitor
- Red (0-39) — Intervene immediately

## Onboarding Framework

The first 90 days determine retention.

### Day 1-7: Activation
- Welcome call within 24 hours of signup
- Set up core product (1-2 key workflows only)
- Define success criteria with the customer
- Assign a named CSM or success resource

### Day 8-30: Value realization
- First check-in: are they using the product?
- Unblock any friction in the core workflow
- Introduce 1-2 additional features tied to their goals
- Share quick-win case study from a similar customer

### Day 31-90: Habit formation
- Weekly usage review (internal)
- Mid-onboarding check-in call
- Identify power users and champion the relationship
- Begin documenting ROI metrics for QBR

**Onboarding red flags:**
- No login after 7 days → immediate outreach
- Only 1 user active on a team seat → adoption problem
- No response to 3 outreach attempts → escalate to account owner

## QBR Framework (Quarterly Business Review)

Structure for high-touch accounts:

1. **Business recap** — Their goals from last quarter, what happened
2. **Usage & ROI** — Your metrics that tie to their outcomes
3. **Wins** — Specific value delivered (with numbers)
4. **Gaps** — Where they haven't gotten full value yet
5. **Roadmap** — Upcoming features relevant to their goals
6. **Next quarter** — Mutual success plan with owners and dates

**QBR anti-patterns:**
- Showing your metrics (logins, seats) without tying to their outcomes
- No customer talking — it should be 50/50
- No clear next steps with owners

## Expansion Playbook

Expand after proven value, not before.

**Triggers for expansion conversation:**
- Customer hits usage limit or tier ceiling
- New use case discovered in QBR
- Champion gets promoted or changes roles
- Customer references you to another team
- NPS score of 9-10

**Expansion plays:**
- **Seat expansion** — Identify unused seats + teams that would benefit
- **Feature upsell** — Map premium features to their stated goals
- **New department** — Land and expand into adjacent team
- **Multi-year** — Offer pricing incentive for annual/multi-year commit

## Churn Prevention

**Early warning system — intervene at first signal:**

| Signal | Action | Timeline |
|--------|--------|----------|
| No login 14 days | Automated check-in email + CSM alert | Same day |
| Submitted cancellation request | Executive sponsor outreach | <2 hours |
| NPS score 0-6 | Personalized call from CSM | <24 hours |
| Support escalation | CSM joins support thread | Same day |
| Key champion leaves | Map new stakeholder immediately | <48 hours |
| Renewal <60 days + no engagement | Full save play | Immediately |

**The save conversation:**
1. Acknowledge the issue without being defensive
2. Ask what success would look like for them
3. Propose a concrete fix with a timeline
4. Offer a concession only if necessary (not as a first move)
5. Set a 30-day check-in to verify

## NPS / CSAT Program

**NPS cadence:**
- Trigger: 30 days after activation, then every 90 days
- Detractors (0-6): Personal outreach within 24 hours
- Passives (7-8): Ask what would move them to a 9
- Promoters (9-10): Ask for a referral or case study

**Closing the loop:**
Every NPS response needs an action logged. Detractors who get a follow-up call are 4x more likely to renew.

## Customer Advocacy

**Advocacy ladder:**
1. Happy customer → Ask for G2/Capterra review
2. Willing to share results → Case study or data quote
3. Champion with network → Referral program
4. Power user → Advisory board or beta tester
5. Evangelist → Conference speaker or co-marketing

**Ask timing:** After a win, never after a problem.
