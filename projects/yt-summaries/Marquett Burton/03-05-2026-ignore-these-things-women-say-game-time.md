## The Core Tactics Women Run (What to Ignore)

### 1. "You Need to Get to Know Me"
This is one of the main themes of the entire video.

When a woman says "get to know me" or "you don't even know me" — she doesn't actually want you to understand her. She wants to:
- Hold your **time and attention hostage**
- Make you talk about her / listen to her talk about herself
- Get you to see her through **rose-colored glasses** (the way she sees herself), not reality

What she wants you to "know" about her is a curated fiction: that she's clean (she's not), that she can cook (she can't), that she's loyal (she's talking to her ex). It's brainwashing, not intimacy.

**What to do:** Don't engage the process. You already know everything you need to know from her behavior. Action reveals character faster than any conversation.

### 2. The Virgin Card
Women claim virginity to gain power/leverage over the interaction. He's heard it countless times.

His approach:
- Give her a genuine chance to recant: *"Sometimes we want people to think well of us and we exaggerate. I'm not here to judge — you can restate that."*
- If she persists, accept it without changing your behavior or valuation
- He doesn't believe it, doesn't factor it in either way
- Being a virgin is not a skill set. What matters is what she can do, not what she claims not to have done

**Key line:** *"If you are a virgin and you have the basic womanly skills — you can cook, clean, follow directions, accept no — we could stick together for a lifetime. The requirements aren't that high. It's just that women are so low."*

### 3. "I've Never Done This Before"
Statistically implausible. A woman in her 20s who grew up in the current era, dated multiple men, went to parties, drank — the chances she's never done standard things are essentially zero. Don't entertain it.

### 4. Lying and Imprecise Language
Women use imprecise language deliberately to confuse you. When caught in a lie, they persist — at such a convincing level you start questioning yourself.

**Never question yourself.** They are consummate actresses and master emotional manipulators. Cut the conversation before they can open their manipulation bag. 

*"Stop it there. I accept what you're saying."* — This is his line. He stops her before she can elaborate, make excuses, or spin the story.

### 5. Shifting Blame
When a woman is caught being unreliable, her next move is to frame your standards as the problem. Example: he calls her out for being flaky → she responds "Schedules, rules, agreements, business-like relationships. There's no flow."

She's not taking responsibility. She's trying to make your expectations the issue so she escapes consequence.

**What to do:** Don't argue. Don't engage. Don't get petty. Just exit. *"You can't get me to argue with you. You got to go."*

### 6. Trying to Extend Conversations You've Ended
When you say bye — that means the conversation is over. If she keeps talking, asks "why," tries to slip in "one more thing" — that's a test of your leadership. She's checking if you'll hold your frame.

Hold it. If needed, hang up. Don't explain yourself. *"I don't need a reason to get off the phone with you. I'm done talking."*

She will frame it as rudeness. It's not. Her prolonging the conversation after you've closed it is the actual disrespect.

### 7. Asking the Same Question Twice
She already heard your answer. She's asking again hoping for a different one. Don't repeat yourself. Don't explain. Your first answer stands. Repeating yourself signals your word isn't final.

### 8. Excess Texts / Voice Memos
Women send 10 texts or 5 voice memos when one would do. He listens to one, if that. His reply: an emoji, a reaction icon, a thumbs up/down.

When she says *"You have to stop replying in emojis, I need a real response"* — that IS the real response. If he were in person and she said something dumb, he'd side-eye her. The emoji IS the side-eye.

Bonus function: emoji replies filter out the most idiotic ones. The women with no substance can't sustain a connection where they're getting emojis back. They self-select out.

### 9. Blood-Sucking Your Time
Women will always try to take more of your time than they've earned. The amount of time you give should be proportional to the value they provide.

*"If you offered more value, you'd get more time. The amount of time I give you is what you're worth."*

Limiting your time is also conditioning. It teaches her what's acceptable. She sits on the bench, reflects, and gets back in the game with a better approach — or she doesn't, and you move on.

### 10. Fake Holidays / Gift Extraction
Valentine's Day, birthdays — women use these to extract gifts and attention, while rarely reciprocating. They dump you right before YOUR birthday so they don't have to give anything.

His stance: he hasn't bought a Valentine's gift or birthday gift for a woman in he can't remember when. He gives zero kudos. You have to *show him something* to earn praise, not just exist.

### 11. Demanding to Be Picked Up
Women expect you to drive to them, pick them up, and shuttle them around. His response: *"I don't drive Uber."*

If they want to see him, they come to him. He meets them downstairs at a set time. That's the offer. They fall in line when they realize he's not playing games.

### 12. "You Don't Put in Effort"
She hasn't cooked you a meal. She hasn't served you. She hasn't done anything to earn your effort yet. Effort should flow in response to demonstrated value — not as a prerequisite to it. She needs to earn the effort, not demand it upfront.

### 13. Sex Theatrics / Faking Tightness / Fake Pain

He calls it out directly: *"You're an actress right now and I don't appreciate it. Don't insult my intelligence."*

When she leg-locks you — it's not tightness, it's her thigh grip. When she claims she bled — unless you genuinely went to town, she should see a gynecologist. He's experienced enough to tell the difference between genuine and performance.
