## Core Framework: How to Spot a Profitable App Idea

**Find where money already moves, but the process is broken.**

Not "what sounds cool" — find a transaction that already happens at scale that is painful, manual, or leaking money.

**4 signals to look for:**
1. High ticket value per transaction
2. Bad or missing automation
3. Accuracy gaps (stale/wrong data)
4. Humans doing what a script should do

## Case Study: SkyAccess (skyaccess.com)

Private aviation marketplace for empty-leg flights. No membership, no brokers.

**Live pricing shown on stream:**
- Beaufort → Fort Pierce: $2,963
- Hilton Head → Miami: $3,015–$7,243
- Savannah → Fort Lauderdale: $9,447
- Hilton Head → Miami (market median): $12,469 — SkyAccess price: $3,105

**Key slide:** "$12,000 for a 1.5-hour empty leg Savannah to Miami"
The gap between market rate and listed price = the value proposition.

---

## Where SkyAccess Breaks Down (the opportunity)

- **No follow-up after first booking** — no automated retention
- **No follow-up after second booking** — then price changes (customer confusion)
- **Message sent to wrong person** — operator notification accidentally exposed: Route KSAV→KCPP, Departure May 1 2026 11:03 AM, Tail N760JP, Listed Rate $9,050
- **Listing accuracy is poor** — stale or incorrect flight info
- **Humans doing manual outreach** — calling operators to verify listings

---

## Improvement Roadmap Slide (Marquett's 4-Point Plan)

1. Copy tech
2. Automate communications/bookings
3. **Improve accuracy of listings** (highlighted)
4. AI or cheap employee calling to do footwork quickly

## Key Takeaway

The thesis: find a marketplace with high-value inventory (empty-leg jets at 75% off), broken UX, zero automation, and no follow-up. Build the better version or build the automation layer on top of it. The product doesn't have to be new — it has to be better at converting what already exists.
